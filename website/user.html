Emperor Penguins are the largest species of penguins, and they are found only in Antarctica. They have distinctive black and white plumage, with a black head, back, tail, and wings, and a white belly and chest. They have a thick layer of blubber and feathers to keep them warm in the extremely cold temperatures of their habitat.

They are the largest of all penguin species

The social behaviour is also associated with the name "emperor".

The females lay a single egg and then pass it off to the males to keep warm while they go off to feed.

Emperor Penguins do have ears, but they do not have external ear openings.

Instead, their ears are located behind their eyes and are covered by feathers.

Emperor Penguins mainly feed on fish, krill, and squid.

They catch while swimming and diving in the icy waters of Antarctica.

They form large groups and have a highly organized breeding cycle.
